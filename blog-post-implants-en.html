What Are Dental Implants?

Dental implants replace missing teeth with artificial ones that look and function just like natural teeth. An implant consists of three parts:

Illustration of dental implant components
1 The Implant
2 The Abutment
3 The Crown

1. The Implant: A small medical-grade titanium screw implanted into the jawbone that acts as an artificial root for the tooth. Titanium is highly biocompatible — meaning the bone naturally fuses around it and holds it firmly.

2. The Abutment: A small connector piece attached to the top of the implant after it heals with the bone. It acts as a connecting ring between the implant and the crown.

3. The Crown: The visible tooth you see in your mouth. Made of porcelain or zirconia and designed to perfectly match the color and shape of your natural teeth.

Traditional vs. Immediate Dental Implants

Traditional Implants:

In the traditional method, the process goes through several stages taking from 3 to 6 months:

  • First Stage: Extracting the damaged tooth (if present) and waiting for the gums to heal (4-8 weeks)
  • Second Stage: Placing the implant in the bone and waiting for healing (3-6 months)
  • Third Stage: Attaching the abutment and final crown

Immediate Implants:

This is what most patients ask about — and rightly so! In Immediate Implantation the tooth is extracted and the implant is placed in the same session, and in many cases a temporary crown can also be attached on the same day.

The Result: You enter the clinic with a broken or missing tooth and leave on the same day with a new tooth!

Who Is the Ideal Candidate for Dental Implants?

Dental implants are suitable for most healthy adults, but certain conditions must be met:

An ideal candidate if you:

  • Are an adult (18 years or older) with fully grown jawbones
  • Enjoy good general health
  • Have sufficient bone density in the jaw (or are willing to undergo a bone graft)
  • Do not suffer from uncontrolled chronic diseases
  • Are a non-smoker (or are willing to quit)
  • Are committed to oral hygiene and regular visits

Implants may not suit you if you:

  • Have uncontrolled diabetes
  • Take medications that affect bone healing
  • Are a heavy smoker unwilling to quit
  • Have severe jawbone loss (in some cases can be solved with a bone graft)
💡
Dr. Ahmed's Advice:

"Smoking is the most negative factor affecting implant success. If you are a smoker and considering implants, I advise you to stop at least two weeks before the procedure and two months after. This alone significantly increases your implant's success rate."

How Is Dental Implant Surgery Performed? — Step by Step

I will explain exactly what happens at each stage so you know what to expect:

Step 1: Examination & Digital Planning

This is the most important stage — and what distinguishes advanced clinics from others.

We begin with digital panoramic and 3D CBCT scans of your entire jaw. These images provide us with a precise, detailed map of the jawbone, nerves, and sinuses.

Using digital planning software, we determine with extreme precision:

  • The ideal implant location
  • The correct angle
  • The appropriate depth
  • The type and size of implant suited for your case

At Pearl Smile Clinic: We use a 3D digital planning system that allows us to perform the procedure virtually on the computer before touching your mouth — which significantly increases the accuracy and success rate.

Step 2: Anesthesia & Site Preparation

Surgery day — and it's easier than you imagine!

We start with local anesthesia. In our clinic we use a digital anesthesia device that delivers the anesthetic slowly and precisely — most of our patients say they "didn't feel a thing".

If you have a damaged tooth that needs extracting, we gently extract it first.

Step 3: Placing the Implant

Using a pre-fabricated digital surgical guide, we open a small space in the jawbone and place the implant exactly in the digitally planned location and angle.

The procedure itself takes only 15-30 minutes per implant.

You will not feel any pain — just light pressure.

Step 4: Temporary Crown (in Immediate)

If your case is suitable for immediate implantation (which the doctor determines based on the implant's stability in the bone), we attach a temporary crown in the same session.

You leave the clinic with a new tooth on the same day!

Step 5: Healing Period

Over 3-6 months, something amazing happens: your jawbone grows around the implant and fuses with it in a process called Osseointegration. This is what makes the implant as stable and strong as a natural root.

During this period, the temporary crown works perfectly — you eat, speak, and smile naturally.

Step 6: Final Crown

After healing is complete, we take a digital impression with a 3D scanner (no messy putty!) and design the final crown from porcelain or zirconia.

The final crown is custom-made to match your teeth in color, shape, and size — no one will be able to tell the difference between it and your natural teeth.

Are Dental Implants Painful?

This is what worries patients the most — and my short answer is: No.

This might sound strange, but dental implant surgery is less painful than extracting a wisdom tooth! The reason:

During the procedure:

  • Local anesthesia prevents pain entirely
  • In our clinic we use digital anesthesia delivered slowly — you won't even feel the needle prick
  • العملية نفسها تستغرق ١٥-٣٠ دقيقة فقط

After the procedure:

  • Mild to moderate pain for 2-3 days (like pain after an extraction)
  • Completely controlled with regular painkillers (Panadol or Ibuprofen)
  • Most of our patients return to their normal lives the very next day
  • Mild swelling in the cheek that disappears within 3-5 days

What Is the Success Rate of Dental Implants?

Dental implants are among the most successful dental procedures ever:

  • Global Success Rate: 95-98%
  • Success Rate in our clinic: Over 98% (thanks to digital planning and high-quality implants)
  • Implant Lifespan: With proper care, they last a lifetime

Factors increasing success rate:

  • Choosing a highly experienced specialist doctor
  • Using implants from certified global companies

  • Patient compliance with post-operative instructions
  • Non-smoking
  • Regular follow-ups with the doctor

Factors decreasing success rate:

  • Smoking (reduces the rate to 85-90%)
  • Uncontrolled chronic diseases (diabetes, osteoporosis)
  • Poor oral hygiene
  • Choosing cheap, unknown implants

Post-Surgery Dental Implant Care

First Week After Implant:

  • Eat only soft, cold foods (yogurt, lukewarm soup, juices, boiled eggs)
  • Avoid chewing on the implant side
  • Do not smoke at all
  • Do not spit forcefully and do not use a straw
  • Brush your teeth gently with a soft brush (avoid the implant area directly)
  • Use the medical mouthwash prescribed by the doctor
  • Ice packs on the outside of the cheek (20 mins on / 20 mins off) in the first 48 hours
  • Take prescribed medications regularly (antibiotics + painkillers + anti-inflammatories)

First 3 Months:

  • Avoid very hard foods (hard nuts, ice, hard candy)
  • Brush your teeth twice daily with flossing
  • Commit to follow-up appointments with the doctor
  • Inform the doctor immediately if you feel any movement in the implant or increasing pain

Long-Term:

  • Treat your implants like your natural teeth — clean them daily
  • Visit the dentist every 6 months for checkups and cleaning
  • Use an interdental brush around the implant
  • Do not bite hard objects (pens, nails, opening bottles)
  • If you grind your teeth at night, use a night guard
